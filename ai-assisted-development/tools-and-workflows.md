This document tracks how I currently use AI and how I plan to expand usage responsibly while learning Azure and Cloud.

## 1) Current Tools

### ChatGPT Plus

**Status:** Active use.

**How I use it today:**

- Clarify Azure and Cloud concepts in plain language.
- Break large topics into step-by-step study plans.
- Generate practice questions and self-check prompts.
- Improve technical writing quality (notes, summaries, retrospectives).
- Draft alternatives when I get stuck on wording or structure.

### OpenWhisper (voice transcription)

**Status:** Active use.

**How I use it today:**

- Convert spoken reflections into written notes.
- Capture answers to Socratic-style mentoring questions.
- Reduce friction when documenting lessons learned after labs.

## 3) Usage Principles

I use the following principles to keep AI helpful and responsible:

1. **AI as copilot, not autopilot.**
   - AI can suggest and accelerate, but I keep ownership of decisions.

2. **Understanding first, speed second.**
   - I do not accept output I cannot explain in my own words.

3. **Verify before applying.**
   - I cross-check commands, architecture suggestions, and security implications.

4. **Document assumptions and trade-offs.**
   - When AI proposes options, I capture why I chose one approach.

5. **Prefer small, reviewable steps.**
   - I ask for incremental changes rather than large opaque outputs.

6. **Preserve my learning loop.**
   - I use AI to support deliberate practice, not to skip fundamentals.

## 4) How AI Supports My Learning

In my Azure/Cloud mentoring process, AI helps in specific ways:

- **Pre-study structuring:**
  - turn broad topics into focused study sessions.

- **Concept reinforcement:**
  - re-explain difficult ideas from different angles until they click.

- **Socratic practice:**
  - generate follow-up questions that test reasoning, not memorization.

- **Lab debriefing:**
  - transform raw notes/transcriptions into clear lessons learned.

- **Communication quality:**
  - improve clarity, concision, and professionalism in documentation.

## 5) Guardrails (What I do not outsource to AI)

To avoid shallow learning or risky decisions, I do **not** outsource:

- Final technical judgment.
- Security-sensitive decisions without independent verification.
- Architecture decisions without understanding requirements and constraints.
- Incident/troubleshooting conclusions without evidence from logs/metrics.
- Personal learning accountability (I still do the hands-on work).
